Sc. 04 / The model
Not an AI tool.
A new production house.

Every M·ARK module ships with two layers — a domain-expert creative who has done the job, and an agentic AI built for that job. The creative makes the call. The AI does the work nobody wanted to do anyway. AI doesn’t replace the producer’s taste — it gives them more time to use it.

01
Embedded creatives

Each module is owned by a creative who has done the job at scale — not a PM, not a designer with a moodboard. A line producer owns the budget. A 1st AD owns the call sheet. A production designer owns the breakdown. Their taste is the product.

02
Agentic search

We crawl the production web wide and far — rate cards, union schedules, vendor catalogues, weather windows, location permits, equipment houses. The AI brings back the answer the producer would have spent half a day googling. Every decision shows its sources.

03
Proprietary datasets

Market-specific local data the rest of the AI stack doesn’t have. Indian Cinema rate cards. UK commercial vendor maps. Music-video crew rosters. Built from real call sheets and real budgets we’ve worked on. The model knows what an Alexa 35 actually rents for in Mumbai vs Manchester.

04
RL environments

Every production that runs through M·ARK feeds back. What came in under budget. What overran. Which line items the producer cut. Which vendors delivered. The system trains on outcomes — not opinions — from the productions our creatives actually shipped.

“The most personal is the most creative. Humans don’t scale; AI doesn’t taste. Put them in the same room and you get production support that didn’t exist before.”
— The M·ARK working principle
Sc. 05 / How it works
Slate. Wire. Ship.
With a human in the cut.
01
Slate the production
Drop in the script, the dates, the lead cast — or just paste a paragraph. The agentic layer sluglines the scenes, counts the days, builds the breakdown. The line producer on duty checks it before you see it.
02
Wire the floor
Plug in Gmail, WhatsApp, Drive. Crew, vendors, location managers, NDAs — M·ARK holds the rolodex and the paper trail. Our 1st AD on the module makes sure the call sheet reads like a call sheet, not a SaaS export.
03
Ask, and it ships
“Cut tomorrow’s call sheet.” “Trim catering by ₹2L.” “Send the new SOC to all 40 crew.” English or Hindi. The AI does the busywork; the human reviews anything you’d want a human to review.
